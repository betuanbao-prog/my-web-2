About Pickleball

Overview

Pickleball is the fastest-growing sport in North America, combining elements of tennis, badminton, and table tennis. Invented in 1965 as a backyard game for families, pickleball has evolved into a competitive sport with professional tournaments and millions of players worldwide. Played on a court smaller than tennis, with a solid paddle and a plastic ball with holes, pickleball offers accessibility to players of all ages and skill levels while providing exciting competitive opportunities.

Key Rules

Pickleball is played on a court 44 feet long and 20 feet wide, with a net 36 inches high. Two or four players use solid paddles to hit a plastic ball with holes. The serve must be underhand and diagonal, and the ball must clear the net and land in the service box. Players score points only on their serve (side-out scoring in some formats). A key rule is the non-volley zone (kitchen), a 7-foot area on each side of the net where players cannot hit the ball in the air. First team to 11 points (win by 2) typically wins the game.
