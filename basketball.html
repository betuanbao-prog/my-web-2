About Basketball

Overview

Basketball is a dynamic team sport played by millions globally. Invented in 1891 by James Naismith, the game has evolved into one of the most exciting and widely watched sports in the world. Basketball combines speed, athleticism, skill, and tactical intelligence. Whether played in professional leagues like the NBA or recreational courts worldwide, basketball is known for its fast pace, high-flying plays, and ability to captivate audiences with thrilling performances and fierce competition.

Key Rules

Basketball is played between two teams of five players each on a 94-foot court with a 10-foot-high basket at each end. The objective is to score points by shooting the ball through the opponent's hoop. Players can dribble the ball to move around the court, but traveling (taking too many steps without dribbling) is a violation. A game consists of four 12-minute quarters (in the NBA) or two 20-minute halves (in college). Free throws worth 1 point, field goals worth 2 points, and three-pointers worth 3 points.
