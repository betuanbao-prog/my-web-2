About Badminton

Overview

Badminton is an ancient sport with roots dating back centuries, now played competitively by millions worldwide. Known for its incredible speed, precision, and tactical depth, badminton has evolved from a leisure activity into one of the most technically demanding racquet sports. The sport emphasizes agility, hand-eye coordination, and strategic thinking. From recreational play in backyards to professional tournaments on the world stage, badminton captivates audiences with breathtaking rallies and displays of extraordinary athleticism.

Key Rules

Badminton is played on a rectangular court 44 feet long and 20 feet wide (doubles) or 17 feet wide (singles). Players use racquets to hit a shuttlecock (birdie) over a net 30 inches high. The objective is to hit the shuttlecock so the opponent cannot return it. Points are scored when the opponent fails to return the shuttlecock or commits a fault. A match typically consists of 2 out of 3 games, with games played to 21 points (win by 2). The serve must be underhand and diagonal across the net.
